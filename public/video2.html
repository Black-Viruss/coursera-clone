4Cs Synergies Framework

An operational synergy exists if two businesses operated jointly are more valuable than the two businesses operated independently.

We distinguish between four synergy operators (referred to as the “4Cs”) that can be applied to the value chains of two businesses. We can describe the 4Cs along two dimensions: the similarity of the resources underlying the value chain activities being liked (low or high) and the extent of modification of the resources underlying these value chain activities that is necessary for value creation (low or high).

1. Consolidation: Modify similar resources to eliminate redundancy (high similarity of resources, high modification of resources)

2. Combination: Pool similar resources to achieve scale (high similarity of resources, low modification of resources)

3. Customization: Modify different resources to make them work better together (low similarity of resources, high modification of resources)

4. Connection: Pool dissimilar resources to facilitate their joint use (low similarity of resources, low modification of resources.)

Examples are:

1. Consolidation:
· Reducing headcount by merging departments, where the same work is done by fewer people.

· Closing factories and thus reducing the capital invested.

2. Combination:
· Obtaining volume discounts from increased procurement volume and thus increase bargaining power with suppliers.

· Increasing political influence from a larger size.

3. Customization:
· Creating a customized bundle of products or services (“solutions”) to meet the needs of particular clients.

· Building a dedicated warehouse with spare parts next to a manufacturing site to minimize delivery times.

4. Connection:
· Cross-selling products to each others’ customers.

· Applying a common brand across different businesses.
